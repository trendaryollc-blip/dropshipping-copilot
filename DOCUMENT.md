# Trendaryo.com User Guide

This guide explains how to use every main feature of your site in plain language. It is written for someone who has never used this kind of tool before.

---

## 1. What this site is for

Your site is a dropshipping business dashboard. It helps you:
- find products to sell,
- find suppliers who can send products for you,
- write product descriptions using AI,
- manage orders,
- see business data and reports,
- and connect your online stores so everything works together.

This site is live at `trendaryo.com` and deployed with Vercel. Your code is hosted on GitHub, and your domain name is managed with Cloudflare.

> Note: the code in this project uses some example data and tools. Some screens show sample information to help you understand how the system will work.

---

## 2. Basic website pages and how to use them

### Dashboard

The dashboard is the first page you see when you log in. It shows a quick summary of your business:
- Products imported into the system
- Suppliers connected
- Orders waiting to be handled
- Monthly revenue estimates

How to use it:
1. Look at the number cards to see your current business picture.
2. Click links for quick actions such as `Find Products`, `Find Suppliers`, `Generate Description`, or `Track Orders`.
3. Read the `Beginner Tip` box to learn one simple idea for improving your store.

### Product search page

This page helps you find items that can sell well.
- You can search by product name or category.
- The site shows product ideas and how good they are for dropshipping.

How to use it:
1. Type a product or keyword into the search box.
2. Review the product cards that appear.
3. Choose products with a high trend score and low competition.
4. Save the products you want to sell.

### Supplier finder page

This page helps you find suppliers.
- Suppliers are the companies that send the products to customers.
- The page shows supplier names and basic supplier information.

How to use it:
1. Open the supplier page.
2. Read the supplier details.
3. Choose suppliers that look reliable, with good reviews and fast shipping.
4. Save or connect the supplier to your system.

### Product description page

This is an AI page. It helps you write product descriptions automatically.
- It uses smart software (AI) to write text.
- You give the AI the product name, features, audience, and tone.

How to use it:
1. Choose the product you want to describe.
2. Enter what the product is, who will buy it, and the tone you want.
3. Click button to create the description.
4. Read the result and use it on your product page.

### Orders page

This page helps you track customer orders.
- It shows orders that are pending, shipped, delivered, or cancelled.
- You can see status updates like `Pending` or `Processing`.

How to use it:
1. Open the orders page.
2. Review each order status.
3. Update orders when you ship them or receive customer information.

### Analytics page

Analytics means looking at numbers to understand how your store is doing.
- This page shows sales data and store performance.
- It helps you see which products sell best.

How to use it:
1. Open analytics to see performance charts.
2. Look for products or areas that are doing well.
3. Use this information to decide what to promote or improve.

### SEO page

SEO is how your products appear in internet searches.
- This page helps you improve product listings so customers can find them.
- It shows keywords and ideas to make your product pages better.

How to use it:
1. Open the SEO page.
2. Look at the recommended words and phrases.
3. Add those words to your product title and description.

### Mobile page

This page is for using the dashboard from your phone.
- It may show a mobile-friendly view of your system.

How to use it:
1. Open the mobile page to check how the site looks on a phone.
2. Use it to review key information when you are away from a computer.

### Multi-store page

This page helps if you use more than one online store.
- It shows all your connected stores in one place.

How to use it:

### My Products page

This page shows products you have added or imported.

## 3. How to connect your store and suppliers

### Connecting your online store

Your store is where customers buy your products. Connecting your store means the dashboard can talk to it.

How to use the connect page:
1. Go to `Connect Your Stores` or the integration page.
2. Find the button for your store type, for example `Shopify`.
3. Enter the store address and any required key.
4. Click `Connect`.

If your store is live on `trendaryo.com`, the site can show sales and product data.

### Connecting suppliers

This means the app can retrieve product information from the supplier.
- The supplier gives you product inventory and price details.
- It can also help you place orders or request quotes.

How to connect:
1. Open the supplier connection page.
2. Find `Connect Supplier API`.
3. Enter the API key and secret provided by the supplier.
4. Click `Connect`.

After connecting, the page shows that the supplier is `Connected and syncing`.

> Important: In this code, supplier connection is simulated. It looks like a real connect flow, but it uses example data unless real supplier API code is added.

## 4. AI features explained for beginners

### What is AI?

AI stands for `artificial intelligence`. It is a computer tool that can read information, think a little like a person, and then help you make decisions.

In your site, AI is used for:
- finding the best products,
- helping you write product descriptions,
- suggesting better prices,
- analyzing competition,
- and helping you find suppliers.

### How AI works on this site

The site uses a service called `OpenAI`.
- OpenAI is a company that makes smart text tools.
- The site sends a question to OpenAI and receives a response with recommendations.

If the site has a real OpenAI key, the AI answers from the internet and gives real suggestions.
If there is no key, the app shows example answers.

### How to connect AI

To connect AI, you need a key from OpenAI.

1. Sign up on the OpenAI website.
2. Find your API key in your account.
3. Add the key to your site settings in Vercel as an environment variable:
   - `NEXT_PUBLIC_OPENAI_API_KEY`
4. Redeploy the site on Vercel.

Once your key is live, the AI pages will send your questions to OpenAI and get real responses.

### AI features in simple terms

- `Analyze Product`: The AI reads a product name and tells you whether it is a good product to sell.
- `Generate Description`: The AI writes the product text for your product page.
- `Optimize Price`: The AI suggests a better price based on the product category and competitor data.
- `Analyze Competition`: The AI looks at similar products and tells you about top competitors.

### Using AI for supplier finding

AI can help you find suppliers by:
1. Entering the product or supplier information.
2. Asking the site to look for products with a strong chance to sell.
3. Getting suggestions on which suppliers are likely to be good.

If the app is connected to real supplier data, AI can use that data to recommend suppliers faster.

## 5. Deploying and updating your site

Because your site is live on Vercel:
- the code must be on GitHub,
- Vercel reads the code from GitHub,
- Cloudflare sends visitors to the Vercel site.

How updates work:
1. Make a change in your code locally or in GitHub.
2. Push the code to GitHub.
3. Vercel automatically rebuilds the site.
4. Your live site at `trendaryo.com` updates.

### Firestore note

Your site uses Firestore for data storage.
- Firestore is a Google database that saves your products, orders, and settings.
- If the site is connected correctly, the live data is stored in Firestore.

In this project, many screens show sample or mock data. To make everything fully live, the app should connect the UI pages to Firestore data.

## 7. Simple glossary of terms

- `Store`: Your online shop where customers buy products.
- `Supplier`: The company that sends the product to a customer.
- `Product`: An item you sell.
- `Order`: A customer purchase.
- `Dashboard`: The main page with your business summary.
- `AI`: Smart computer tool that helps with writing and analysis.
- `API`: A way software talks to another service.
- `Vercel`: The company that runs your live website.
- `Cloudflare`: The company that manages your website address.
- `Firestore`: The database that saves your store data.

## 8. What to do if something is not working

If a page does not show real data:
- it may be using sample data,
- it may need the Firestore connection to be finished,
- or it may need the OpenAI key to be added.

If the supplier connection does not work:
- make sure the supplier gave you the correct API key and secret,
- and check if the app is using a real supplier API.

If AI answers are not showing real results:
- add the OpenAI key to the Vercel environment,
- then redeploy the site.
